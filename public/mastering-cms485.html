Risk Analysis

Why Claims Get Denied

An incomplete 485 is a liability. Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs) frequently deny payment for technical deficiencies.

  • Unspecific Frequency: "PRN" orders without specific qualifiers are invalid.
  • Missing Signatures: Physician signature not dated prior to claim submission.
  • Goal Mismatch: Goals do not match the identified deficits in OASIS.
  • Weak Skilled Rationale: Insufficient evidence of skilled need in visit notes.
  • Homebound Not Verified: Generic homebound statements without clinical detail.

Composition

Anatomy of the 485

A compliant Plan of Care requires specific data elements. Missing any one renders the document incomplete.

Required Data Elements

1. Medical Necessity

Must clearly state the primary diagnosis and the specific skilled need (wound care, gait training, etc.).

2. Medications & Treatments

A complete list of all medications (Rx and OTC) and specific treatments/supplies.

3. Functional Limitations

Specific limitations (Amputation, Dyspnea, etc.) that support homebound status.

4. Measurable Goals

Goals must be realistic, patient-specific, and measurable with a target date.

5. Safety & DME

Safety precautions, durable medical equipment, nutritional needs, and homebound justification.
